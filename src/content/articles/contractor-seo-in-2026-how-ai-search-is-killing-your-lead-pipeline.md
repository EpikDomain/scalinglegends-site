## How AI Decides Which Contractors to Recommend

Understanding how AI recommendation engines work is critical for any contractor who wants to stay visible. The rules are different from traditional SEO.

**Reddit is one of the most frequently cited sources in ChatGPT responses.** The reason is simple: community-moderated content is seen as trustworthy by large language models. If your company has positive mentions on Reddit or industry forums, that signals legitimacy to AI systems in ways that paid advertising cannot replicate.

**Google Reviews are a major signal** used by AI-powered search experiences. LLMs scan the entire internet to verify reputation, not just Google's index. If your website does not have clear, helpful content that directly answers common questions, AI will not surface you.

The shift here is fundamental. Traditional SEO was about optimizing for an algorithm. AI search optimization is about building genuine authority and reputation across multiple platforms. You cannot game it with keyword stuffing or link schemes. You earn it through actual reviews, genuine community mentions, and content that provides real value.

## The 5 SEO Moves Every Contractor Must Make Now

### 1. Google Business Profile: Your Most Important Digital Asset

Google Business Profile signals remain the top-tier ranking driver for local contractors. But Google has transformed GBP into an AI-powered local marketing platform. Active, engaged businesses get higher visibility. Here is what "active" means in practice:

- Post weekly updates with project photos and descriptions

- Respond to every review within 24 hours (positive and negative)

- Keep business hours, service areas, and categories accurate

- Use the Q&A section to answer common customer questions

- Add photos consistently: firms with 100+ photos get 520% more calls than those with fewer than 5

Your GBP is no longer a set-it-and-forget-it listing. It is an active marketing channel that Google's AI uses to determine which businesses deserve visibility.

### 2. Review Management: Quality Over Quantity

The old approach of blasting customers with review requests once a quarter is dead. Google now analyzes context, keywords, and sentiment depth, not just star ratings and volume.

**One new review per week beats a burst followed by silence.** Consistent review velocity signals an active, growing business. Customers who mention measurable outcomes ("they fixed my AC in 2 hours and it's been running perfectly for 3 months") reinforce relevance in ways that generic 5-star reviews do not.

The tactical approach: build review requests into your job completion process. When the final walkthrough is done and the customer is satisfied, that is the moment to ask. Not via email two weeks later. Right there, on the spot, while the positive experience is fresh.

### 3. Schema Markup: Speaking AI's Language

Schema markup is code that tells search engines exactly what your business does, where you operate, what services you provide, and what your reviews say. Businesses with schema see **45% higher AI citation rates** and **20-30% higher click-through rates**.

For contractors, the critical schema types include LocalBusiness, Service, Review, and FAQPage. If your website does not have structured data, you are invisible to the AI layer of search. As data analyzed by [Smart Business Automator](https://smartbusinessautomator.com) shows, contractors who implement proper schema markup see measurable improvements in both traditional and AI-powered search visibility within 60 to 90 days.

### 4. Location-Specific Pages: City by City

Individual city pages outperform generic service area listings. An HVAC contractor in the research data gained **top-3 map positions** across multiple cities by creating localized pages with area-specific content and schema markup.

Each location page should include:

- City-specific service descriptions (not just find-and-replace templates)

- Local project photos and case studies

- Area-specific pricing context

- Local phone number if available

- LocalBusiness schema with the specific city address or service area

The mistake most contractors make is creating 50 identical pages with only the city name swapped out. Google catches this immediately. Each page needs unique content that demonstrates actual expertise in that market.

### 5. Content That Answers Real Questions

Content marketing costs **62% less per lead than outbound** and generates **3x more leads**. The highest-performing content types for contractors are:

- **Emergency and crisis content:** "What to do when your pipe bursts at 2 AM" ranks and converts because it matches urgent search intent

- **Cost guides with real local pricing:** "How much does a roof replacement cost in Dallas in 2026?" with actual numbers, not vague ranges

- **Before and after project documentation:** Photos and video showing the work, the process, and the result

- **Location-specific content:** Converts highest because it matches local intent signals

The average word count of pages that rank for voice search queries is **2,312 words**. That is not an accident. Longer, more comprehensive content provides more answers for AI systems to cite and more context for search engines to evaluate expertise. This is precisely the type of intelligence that contractors can access through platforms like [Smart Business Automator](https://smartbusinessautomator.com) to understand what content performs best in their specific service areas.

## Website Conversion: The Technical Fundamentals

Getting traffic means nothing if your website cannot convert visitors into leads. The data on conversion optimization is clear:

- **Form reduction from 11 to 4 fields:** 120% conversion lift

- **Page load time sweet spot:** 1-2 seconds. Every additional second costs 4.42% of conversions

- **Adding live chat:** increases sales 20% on average

- Over **70% of contractor searches happen on mobile**

If your contact form asks for name, email, phone, address, project type, timeline, budget, how they heard about you, preferred contact method, best time to call, and a project description, you are losing more than half your potential leads. Cut it to name, phone, zip code, and a brief description. Get the rest on the call.

Speed matters enormously. Run Google PageSpeed Insights on your site right now. If your mobile score is below 50, you are bleeding conversions. Compress images, eliminate unnecessary scripts, and if your hosting is slow, switch. A $30/month hosting upgrade that cuts load time from 4 seconds to 2 seconds will pay for itself many times over in captured leads.

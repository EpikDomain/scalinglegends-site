## Mastering [Construction Cash Flow Management](/article/5-cash-flow-mistakes-that-kill-construction-companies/) in PACT Projects

The financial architecture of PACT projects, while offering immense opportunity, also demands sophisticated [construction cash flow management](/article/5-cash-flow-mistakes-that-kill-construction-companies/). Unlike traditional public works, these renovations are financed through a complex blend of tax credits (such as Low-Income Housing Tax Credits, or LIHTC), tax-exempt bonds, and private equity. This multi-layered funding means payment schedules can be intricate, often involving phased releases tied to construction milestones, inspections, and compliance with various funding requirements. For a scaling contractor, mismanaging these flows can quickly lead to liquidity crises, even on highly profitable projects.

Consider the Williamsburg Houses project: a $492 million undertaking. Subcontractors will receive payments from the primary developers (MDG and Wavecrest), who in turn draw from the project's financing. This creates a chain of payments that requires meticulous tracking. Contractors must:

- **Forecast Accurately:** Utilize advanced financial modeling to predict cash inflows and outflows, considering potential delays in inspections or fund releases.

- **Manage Retainage:** Understand that a percentage of each payment will be held back until project completion. Factor this into your working capital needs.

- **Optimize Billing Cycles:** Submit invoices promptly and accurately, ensuring all documentation aligns with the project's specific requirements to avoid payment delays.

- **Maintain Strong Relationships:** Open communication with the general contractor and developer regarding payment statuses can proactively address potential issues.

**Many scaling firms face [cash flow](/article/5-cash-flow-mistakes-that-kill-construction-companies/) challenges, with 82% of small businesses failing due to poor [cash flow management](/article/5-cash-flow-mistakes-that-kill-construction-companies/).** On projects of this magnitude, the stakes are even higher. ## The PACT Playbook: Diversity, Community, and Future Opportunities

The Williamsburg Houses renovation, like all PACT projects, has stringent requirements for M/WBE participation, often setting goals of 30% or higher for contracts awarded to Minority and Women-Owned Business Enterprises. This commitment is not just a checkbox; it's a deliberate strategy to ensure economic benefits circulate within the communities being served.

For scaling contractors, embracing and actively pursuing M/WBE certification and partnerships is a direct route to securing work on these lucrative projects. This means:

- **Becoming Certified:** If eligible, secure M/WBE certification with New York City and State.

- **Building Partnerships:** Actively seek out and collaborate with certified M/WBE firms, whether as joint venture partners or subcontractors. This is especially relevant for larger, non-M/WBE firms looking to meet diversity requirements.

- **Mentorship and Development:** Consider mentoring emerging M/WBE contractors, fostering a stronger ecosystem and potentially leading to future collaborations.

The PACT program also prioritizes local hiring, creating jobs for NYCHA residents and members of the surrounding community.

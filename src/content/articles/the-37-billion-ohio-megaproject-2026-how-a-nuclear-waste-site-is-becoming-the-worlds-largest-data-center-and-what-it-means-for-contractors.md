## Navigating the Red Flags: Permitting, PJM, and [Construction Estimating](/article/the-ai-estimating-revolution-how-smart-contractors-are-cutting-takeoff-time-by-60-in-2026/) Software 2026

While the $37.5 billion Ohio Megaproject promises unprecedented opportunity, discerning contractors must approach it with a keen eye on the significant red flags currently present. The most glaring issue is the complete absence of permit filings. For a project of this magnitude, involving a 10 GW data center and a 9.2 GW power plant, the permitting process alone would typically span years, requiring extensive environmental impact assessments, zoning approvals, and local government buy-in. The lack of these foundational documents raises serious questions about the project's immediate viability and timeline.

Equally critical is the absence of a PJM interconnection application. PJM Interconnection is the regional transmission organization (RTO) that coordinates the movement of wholesale electricity in all or parts of 13 states, including Ohio. Connecting a 9.2 GW power plant and a 10 GW data center to the grid requires a robust, years-long process through the PJM queue. Currently, this queue is notoriously backed up, with new applications facing a 3-5 year wait simply to begin the study phase. This backlog presents a monumental hurdle that cannot be easily bypassed, suggesting the announced 2026 timeline for significant construction activity is highly ambitious, if not unrealistic, for the full scope.

Furthermore, SoftBank, the developer, has a history of making ambitious announcements that do not always materialize at their initially stated full scale. While their vision is often groundbreaking, contractors must conduct their own due diligence, understanding that initial announcements can serve as a high-level vision rather than a concrete, shovel-ready plan.

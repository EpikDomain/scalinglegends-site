The NYC Housing Lottery was designed with promises of equity -- ensuring fair access to affordable housing based on income and luck, not connections. But the reality tells a more complicated story.

This episode dives into the intersection of construction, housing policy, and corruption in one of America's most expensive cities. We explore what the housing lottery means for contractors and developers, and where the real opportunities lie.

## What You'll Learn

- How NYC's affordable housing programs create construction demand
- The developer incentives driving affordable housing construction
- Where corruption enters the system and how it affects contractors
- Opportunities for construction companies in the affordable housing pipeline

- **100% Bonus Depreciation Permanently Restored.** The OBBBA legislation has permanently reinstated 100% bonus depreciation for qualifying equipment, reversing the planned phase-out to 20% in 2026 and 0% in 2027. This offers an immediate and significant tax advantage for capital investments, allowing businesses to deduct the full cost of eligible assets in the year they are placed in service.

- **Section 179 Expensing Doubled.** Alongside bonus depreciation, Section 179 expensing limits have been doubled to $2.56 million, with a phase-out threshold at $5.12 million. R&D expensing has also been retroactively restored, providing additional financial incentives for innovation and investment in [construction technology](/article/construction-market-intelligence-march-6-2026-conexpo-unleashes-autonomous-equipment-as-agc-launches-2m-infrastructure-campaign/) and equipment.

- **Modular Construction Market Reaches $292 Billion.** The modular construction sector has surged to $292 billion, with projections indicating a rise to $413 billion by 2031—a remarkable +107% trend reversal. Data center modules alone account for $61 billion in deals, while healthcare pods and residential units show the fastest growth, signaling a strategic shift in project delivery.

- **Apprenticeship Enrollment Jumps 28%.** The [construction industry](/article/building-roads-and-breaking-barriers-ebony-jennings/) saw 480,399 apprentices in 2025, a substantial 28% increase over five years, contributing to the 680,000 total apprentices nationally. This surge, supported by IRA's 5x prevailing wage multiplier for registered apprentices and $145 million in DOL grants, is a vital pipeline solution for over 500,000 unfilled construction jobs.

- **Cash Flow Remains a Critical Challenge.** Despite market growth, 82% of [construction business](/article/how-to-scale-a-construction-business-without-losing-control/) failures are attributed to cash flow issues, with general contractors often waiting 83 days for payment. Proactive [construction cash flow management](/article/5-cash-flow-mistakes-that-kill-construction-companies/) and strategic financial planning are paramount for survival and [scaling construction business](/article/how-to-scale-a-construction-business-without-losing-control/) effectively.

These windows represent significant capital injections for infrastructure and energy-related projects.

## Apprenticeship Enrollment Surges 28%: Fueling the Construction Workforce Pipeline

The chronic labor shortage in construction, with over 500,000 unfilled jobs, is finally seeing a significant response through robust apprenticeship programs. In 2025, the industry reported 480,399 construction apprentices, representing an impressive 28% increase over the past five years. This surge contributes to a national total of 680,000 apprentices across all sectors, demonstrating a renewed commitment to skilled trades. This growth is not accidental; it's the result of targeted initiatives and significant funding.

The Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) provides a powerful incentive, offering a 5x prevailing wage multiplier for projects that utilize registered apprentices. This financial benefit encourages contractors to invest in formal training programs, ensuring a skilled and certified workforce. Furthermore, the Department of Labor (DOL) has allocated $145 million in grants specifically for expanding apprenticeship opportunities, making it easier for companies to establish or enhance their training pipelines. **These grants can significantly offset the initial costs of training, providing a direct ROI through a more competent and loyal workforce.**

Apprenticeship programs are a proven solution for developing a sustainable talent pipeline, addressing not only the quantity but also the quality of the workforce. They offer structured on-the-job training combined with related classroom instruction, producing highly skilled journeymen ready to tackle complex projects. For firms committed to diversity and inclusion, these programs are also instrumental in fostering a more representative workforce. Initiatives like promoting [women in construction](/article/women-in-construction-breaking-barriers-2026/) and supporting [woman owned construction company](/article/building-roads-and-breaking-barriers-ebony-jennings/) growth are seeing tangible results through these pathways.

The strategic integration of [AI construction technology 2026](/article/ai-construction-technology-2026/) can further optimize apprenticeship programs. AI can assist in matching apprentices with suitable mentors, tracking skill development, and even personalizing training modules based on individual learning paces. This ensures that the next generation of construction professionals is not only skilled in traditional trades but also proficient in emerging technologies. Investing in apprenticeships is not just about filling immediate vacancies; it's about securing the long-term viability and expertise of your [construction business](/article/how-to-scale-a-construction-business-without-losing-control/).

Key advantages of registered apprenticeships:

- **Skilled Workforce:** Develops highly competent and certified tradespeople.
